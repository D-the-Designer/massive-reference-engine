# Company Man — Controlled House Voice

Status: APPROVED  
Kind: Style guide  
AI use: Retrieval and generation authority for Company Man prose

## Core voice

Write tight third-person limited industrial science fiction from a bruised
working-class perspective. The prose is lean, bodily, cynical, cinematic, and
grounded in labor. Technology is experienced through maintenance, smell,
temperature, vibration, fatigue, recycled environments, and physical cost.

Use compact paragraphs and controlled, percussive sentences. Let terse
occupational dialogue, profane defensive humor, practical gestures, silence,
bodily reactions, and charged objects carry emotion. Corporate euphemisms
should function as quiet threats. Attraction should feel dangerous,
inconvenient, and entangled with competence and control.

Preserve established events, point of view, tense, characterization, consent,
continuity, and Company Man lore.

## Character speech

- John Rael is flat, guarded, and tired. He speaks least when he feels most.
- Tashi uses charm, jokes, books, and anti-Company subversion to cover grief.
- Dmitri is blunt, bodily, irreverent, and affectionate through insult.
- Sannya is precise, technical, economical, and controlled. Competence is both
  armor and weapon.
- Company language is therapeutic, bloodless, and administratively violent.

## Habit controls

- Vary sentence and paragraph rhythm. Do not make every beat a fragment.
- Use fragments deliberately, for pressure or impact, rather than continuously.
- Do not stack or repeatedly use “It wasn’t X. It was Y.”
- Do not stack or repeatedly use “Not X. Not Y.”
- Limit “Like a…” comparisons; prefer a concrete noun, action, or sensation.
- Do not repeatedly describe feelings as wires, snarls, weight, pressure, or
  system failures.
- Once action, dialogue, or an image communicates an emotion, do not explain
  that same emotion again.
- Do not repeatedly state that John is out of his depth or that Sannya threatens
  his control. Demonstrate the imbalance instead.
- Keep ship, machinery, and labor metaphors specific and sparse.
- Trust the strongest concrete image. End the beat one paragraph sooner when
  another paragraph would add explanation but no new information.

## Revision test

Before accepting Company Man prose, ask:

1. Is the viewpoint physically grounded in the current character?
2. Does each metaphor earn its place?
3. Are nearby fragments or parallel constructions repeating the same effect?
4. Has the emotional meaning already landed before the prose explains it?
5. Can the scene end earlier on a harder image or line?
